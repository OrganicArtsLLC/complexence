# Orientation log — <LAB NAME>

The lightest possible measurement habit: one line per session or per experiment
recording whether orientation went up or down, and roughly why. This is the ordinal
"orientation delta" of labs spec §6.2 — a structured before/after judgment, **not** a
claim to a cardinal number. Precision is not the point; a running record is.

Scale (suggested): `-3 … 0 … +3`. Positive = the system got easier to orient inside
for less effort. Log what a full measure would need later, but don't wait for it.

| Date | Context / EXP | Before → After | Δ | Why (one line) |

## Toward a real Λ (optional, later)

When a delta matters enough to ground, decompose it (labs §6.1). The two factors you
can actually estimate first:

- **P(S | M)** — did acting on the map/artifact predict the system's behavior?
- **C(M)** — what did understanding and using it cost?

Point legibility `L = P(S|M) / C(M)` is the honest core. The full five-factor Λ
(compression, prediction, actionability ÷ distortion, load) stays a comparison
scaffold until a lab shows a proxy tracking a real outcome (`RQ-009`).
